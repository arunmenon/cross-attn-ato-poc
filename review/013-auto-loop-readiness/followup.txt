Design decisions (agreed before coding, captured here for the trail):

  Primary ranking metric:  worst-family HN-FPR (minimize).
                           max(hn_account_recovery, hn_large_purchase, hn_travel)
  Tiebreaker:              mean HN-FPR across families.
  AUC / R@FPR:             sanity gates only ("must not regress badly"),
                           NOT the optimization objective.
  Convergence:             halt when worst-family HN-FPR has not improved
                           by >= 0.005 absolute over the last 4 valid
                           x-attn runs, after at least 6 valid x-attn runs.
  Budget separation:       baselines (cpt_light, lora_text, structured_as_text,
                           event_only) are recorded in experiments.jsonl
                           for Day-3 comparison but excluded from the
                           x-attn sweep budget AND from convergence-count
                           windows.

  Rationale (user): "Hard negatives are legitimate customers who look
  suspicious. If one family is still bad, averaging can hide it. In
  fraud/risk systems, one bad customer segment still matters."

2. Blocker: hard-negative FPR CIs are not implemented.

Status: FIXED.

Action: eval/bootstrap_ci.py grew bootstrap_hard_negative_fpr().

Algorithm: per resample, recompute the decision threshold consistent
with target_fpr=0.01 on that resample, then compute per-family HN-FPR
from the resample's hard-negative records using that threshold. Build
1000 (default) bootstrap samples; report 2.5%/97.5% percentile bounds.
Output:

    {
      "target_fpr": 0.01,
      "per_family": {
        "hn_account_recovery": {"point", "ci_lo", "ci_hi", ...},
        "hn_large_purchase":   {...},
        "hn_travel":           {...}
      },
      "worst_family": {"point", "ci_lo", "ci_hi", ...},
      "mean":         {"point", "ci_lo", "ci_hi", ...}
    }

main() now always emits this block (`hard_negative_fpr_at_1pct`) into
ci_report_<mode>.json alongside the AUC + R@FPR CIs.

Verify (local with synthetic preds, no torch):

    On 2000 synthetic predictions (mix of fraud, hard-negatives, and
    clean legit), the new function returned:
      hn_account_recovery point=0.0500 CI [0.0237, 0.0589]
      hn_large_purchase   point=0.0050 CI [0.0000, 0.0159]
      hn_travel           point=0.0000 CI [0.0000, 0.0000]
      worst point=0.0500 CI [0.0237, 0.0589]
      mean  point=0.0183 CI [0.0104, 0.0206]

    Composites and per-family CIs all look sensible; worst-family CI
    width matches the most-variable family as expected.
