Summary:
The Day-1 writeup pivoted the win condition from AUC (saturated at
1.0) to hard-negative FPR. The conceptual explanation of the
auto-research loop matches that pivot. But the executable loop -
launcher ranking, convergence halt, experiments.jsonl row schema,
bootstrap CIs, and parts of the RUNBOOK - is still wired for the
pre-pivot AUC objective. Task #38 cannot trust this loop to pick the
right leader, stop at the right time, or surface the right evidence
for Day-3 synthesis. Seven findings, four blockers/highs that gate
Task #38 and three doc cleanups that need to land before Day 3 reads
results.

Review findings:

1. Blocker: loop objective still uses saturated AUC, not hard-negative FPR.

The writeup says the Day-3 decision is "hard-negative FPR @ 1%," but
the actual launcher ranks current_best and top_3 by auc_stripped, and
convergence halt also uses AUC-stripped. See
scripts/run_next_experiment.py:282 and
scripts/run_next_experiment.py:502. Given Day-1 AUC saturation, this
can pick the wrong leader or halt while HN-FPR is still moving.

2. Blocker: hard-negative FPR CIs are not implemented.

Synthesis criterion says "below structured-as-text baseline, with
non-overlapping bootstrap CIs." But eval/bootstrap_ci.py:126 only
bootstraps AUC and Recall@FPR, not
hard_negative_fpr_at_decision_threshold_1pct. score_risk.py computes
HN-FPR point estimates, but there's no CI surface for the metric now
being used as the win condition.

3. High: experiments.jsonl rows do not contain the fields the explanation says Day 3 will read.

The explanation says each row has hard-negative FPRs, per-journey
breakdown, actor differential, and CIs. The launcher appends only
AUCs, R@FPR=1%, max gate, wall clock, and leakage status. See
scripts/run_next_experiment.py:652. The richer data exists in per-run
metrics_*.json / ci_report.json, but the agent's state loop reads
recent experiments.jsonl rows first.

4. High: baseline rows vs x-attn budget are inconsistent.

The explanation expects about 16 rows: 4 baselines + 12 x-attn runs.
But max_experiments: 12 counts all successful records, not just
x-attn. See scripts/run_next_experiment.py:254. If the baselines are
recorded through the launcher, the sweep can halt after only 8 x-attn
runs. If baselines are not recorded, the agent loses the comparison
floor.

5. High: top-3 eval target is still split across old and new plans.

The explanation says Task #41 uses the 15k LLM eval as the real
comparison. RUNBOOK still says "Top-3 medium eval" and large eval
afterward, matching the older 50k templated ladder. See
RUNBOOK.md:230. Before auto-loop output is interpreted, align
PLAN/RUNBOOK/AGENT instructions around 15k LLM as primary and 50k
templated as regression-only.

6. Medium: insertion-pattern layer counts in the explanation are wrong.

The actual implementation is every_4 = [12,16,20,24,28,32] = 6 blocks,
every_8 = [12,20,28] = 3 blocks, and late_only = [32,33,34,35] = 4
blocks. See src/model/qwen_xattn_wrapper.py:157 and
src/auto_research/configs/sweep_space.yaml:9. The conceptual write-up
said 8 / 4 / 3.

7. Medium: RUNBOOK still contains a stale unsafe instruction.

The explanation correctly says the launcher owns experiments.jsonl
and sweep_state.yaml, but RUNBOOK says the agent should "append a
one-paragraph summary to experiments.jsonl, update sweep_state.yaml."
See RUNBOOK.md:295. That contradicts
src/auto_research/AGENT_INSTRUCTIONS.md:59.

Bottom line:
The conceptual explanation is right, but the executable loop is still
wired for the pre-pivot AUC objective. Before Task #38, fix launcher
state/ranking/halt logic, add HN-FPR CIs, and clarify whether
max_experiments means total records or x-attn sweep records.

Design decisions for the fix (committed in followup):

- HN-FPR composite for ranking and halt:
  primary    = max(hn_account_recovery_fpr, hn_large_purchase_fpr,
                   hn_travel_fpr)   # worst-family
  tiebreaker = mean of the three
  Rationale (user, layman-language): "hard negatives are legitimate
  customers who look suspicious. If one family is still bad, averaging
  can hide it - travel may always be 0% while account recovery is
  still falsely blocking customers. In fraud/risk systems, one bad
  customer segment still matters."

- AUC and R@FPR demoted to "must not regress badly" sanity gates;
  not the thing being optimized.

- Convergence halt: worst-family HN-FPR improvement < 0.005 absolute
  over the last 4 valid x-attn runs, after at least 6 valid x-attn
  runs. (Same numerical threshold as the AUC-era halt; different
  scale - HN-FPR is 0-10%.)

- Budget separation: baselines are recorded through the launcher (we
  want them in experiments.jsonl for Day-3 comparison) but excluded
  from the x-attn sweep budget and from the convergence-count window.
  The halt logic filters by arm == "xattn".
