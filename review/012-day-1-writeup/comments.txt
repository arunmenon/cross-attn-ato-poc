Status: OPEN, doc wording

Details:
README.md:58 says "PLAN.md assumption falsified" and README.md:72 says the 50k templated eval "Falsified PLAN.md's templated narratives distribution-shape match training premise." The second sentence has a valid core: PLAN.md:9 and PLAN.md:382 do claim the templated eval keeps the distribution shape close to training. The stronger "PLAN.md assumption falsified" framing reads like the plan missed the saturation risk, when the plan actually anticipated it.

Recommendation:
Replace the broad line with something narrower: "Result: saturation discovered; the templated medium/large eval distribution-match assumption failed, and the PLAN.md synthetic-data-saturation risk materialized." Keep the Day-2 metric pivot, but do not imply the whole PLAN premise was false.

3. Minor: The Stage-0 wall-time comparison is not an apples-to-apples hardware conclusion.

Status: OPEN, doc wording

Details:
README.md:48 says Stage-0 took 53 minutes "vs PLAN.md's 3-4 hr H100 projection - Blackwell is faster." The observed 53-minute run is useful, but it was on an RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell community host, while PLAN.md was scoped around a single RunPod H100. Phrase it as an observation, not a benchmark: "Observed Stage-0 training wall time was 53 min on RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell for this config, faster than the PLAN.md H100 time budget; not a controlled H100 comparison." If 3194s excludes merge and eval, say that explicitly.
